Undergraduate student in the Experimental Class of Mathematics and Applied Mathematics at Xi'an Jiaotong University.

I am interested in reinforcement learning, embodied AI, simulation, and the habit of turning scattered technical material into clean, readable notes. Recently I have been building study repositories around RL theory, Isaac Sim basics, and simulation methods from PBD and XPBD to FEM.

My current work also includes a research direction on dual-level DRL plus GA for combinatorial optimization, and a theory-oriented track on Transformer depth, stability, and continuous-depth views.

I am always happy to connect with people working on reinforcement learning, embodied AI, evolutionary computation, simulation, or mathematically grounded ML. If our interests overlap, I would be glad to talk about possible collaboration.

About

My academic background is mathematical, but I like working where concepts become operational. That usually means one of three things for me: clarifying an algorithmic idea, writing a small experiment that makes it visible, or organizing a body of notes so that it can be revisited later without friction.

I currently maintain public repositories that document how I study reinforcement learning, embodied AI, and simulation. The aim is not just to archive information, but to make it easier to learn again.

Selected Projects

Reinforcement learning study notes for Obsidian and GitHub, covering MDPs, value-based methods, policy gradients, trust-region methods, and selected model-based topics.

isaacsim-embodied-basics

Small, focused Isaac Sim exercises for understanding scene setup, camera observations, rigid body and collision behavior, and Franka robot pose reasoning.

simulation-learning-notes

A compact learning route from PBD and XPBD to FEM, with notes, comparison figures, and runnable Python demos.
