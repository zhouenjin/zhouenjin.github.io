Q&A

A few short answers about what I am working on, how I study, and what kinds of collaboration I would be excited about.

What are you currently working on?

I am currently spending most of my time on reinforcement learning, embodied AI, simulation, and mathematically grounded machine learning. Concretely, my recent work includes RL notes, Isaac Sim experiments, and simulation learning material from PBD and XPBD to FEM.

Why do you spend so much time writing notes?

Writing notes is the way I slow complex material down enough to really understand it. I also want my notes to be reusable: not only for myself when I come back later, but also for other students who are trying to get into the same topics.

What kind of problems interest you most?

I am especially drawn to problems where mathematical structure and practical systems meet. That includes policy optimization, simulation-based learning, combinatorial optimization with adaptive methods, and the bridge between theory and implementation.

What should I read first from your public work?

If you are interested in reinforcement learning, I would start from rl-notes. If you are more interested in embodied AI, then isaacsim-embodied-basics is the better entry point. For simulation learning, the simulation-learning-notes repository is the most direct place to start.

Are you open to collaboration?

Yes. I am happy to talk with people working on reinforcement learning, embodied AI, simulation, evolutionary computation, or technical note systems. If you think our interests overlap, feel free to email me.
