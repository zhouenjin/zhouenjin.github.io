Q&A

A few short answers about what I am working on, why I write notes, and the kinds of problems I would like to keep exploring.

What are you working on right now?

Most of my recent time has gone into reinforcement learning, embodied AI, and simulation. In practice, that currently means RL notes, Isaac Sim experiments, and simulation study material from PBD and XPBD to FEM.

Why do you spend so much time writing notes?

Because writing is how I check whether I actually understand something. I also like the idea that a note should still be useful months later, both to myself and to anyone else trying to enter the same topic.

Why mathematics and AI at the same time?

I like problems where mathematical structure meets practical systems. Mathematics gives me a way to think clearly, and AI gives me systems that are rich enough to make that clarity feel necessary.

What kind of problems interest you most?

I am especially drawn to policy optimization, simulation-based learning, combinatorial optimization with adaptive methods, and questions where theory and implementation have to meet in a concrete way.

What do you want to learn more deeply next?

I want to go deeper into reinforcement learning theory, embodied intelligence, differentiable simulation, and the kinds of mathematical tools that make modern machine learning easier to reason about.

Where should I start with your public work?

If you are interested in reinforcement learning, I would start from rl-notes. If you are more interested in embodied AI, then isaacsim-embodied-basics is the better entry point. For simulation learning, the simulation-learning-notes repository is the most direct place to start.

Are you open to collaboration?

Yes. I am happy to talk with people working on reinforcement learning, embodied AI, simulation, evolutionary computation, or technical note systems. If you think our interests overlap, I would be glad to hear from you.
